Welcome to Pathanamthitta

Pathanamthitta district is known as the 'Headquarters of Pilgrimage Tourism', of the Kerala State. Situated near the Western Ghats and bordered by the hills, Pathanamthitta district is a treat to eyes.

It is a land of untouched beauty with pristine rivers, lofty mountain ranges and sprawling coconut groves. The region is parted into a fascinating topography of highlands, midlands and lowlands crafted by three rivers coursing in the town.

The town of Pathanamthitta is also marked by a culture unique to this place as well as an art of metal mirrors i.e. Aranmula Kannadi, handcrafted with intricacy and poise.Bounded by captivating vistas at every step, this bewitching town promise of an experience you never dreamt of.

Places to Visit

Sabarimala

Sabarimala temple is situated on a hilltop (about 3000 feet above sea level)inside the Periyar Tiger Reserve surrounded by mountains and dense forest. Sabarimala is believed to be the place where Ayyappan meditated.Sabarimala Sree Ayyappa Temple is one of the most ancient and prominent Sastha temples in the country

Gavi

Gavi is a small village tucked between tea estates and wide and dense forests. A unique project in itself, the place is a model of eco-tourism site bringing to the world its spellbinding abundance in flora and fauna. Gavi is situated some 14 km away from the famed Periyar Tiger Reserve and is heaven on Earth, quite literally.

Adavi

With a 5-km stretch broadened by the Kallar River, Adavi welcomes you to experience nature at its best. The hauntingly beautiful Adavi is known for its coracle rafting or the bowl boat riding – the first of its kind in Kerala.
